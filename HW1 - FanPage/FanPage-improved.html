About

Arijit Singh, born on April 25, 1987, is a luminary in the realm of Indian playback singing and music composition. His journey in the music industry began in 2005 when he participated in the reality show Fame Gurukul, serving as a launching pad for what would become a stellar career.

In 2011, Arijit made his notable entry into Hindi cinema with the hauntingly beautiful "Phir Mohabbat" from Murder 2, a song that had been recorded in 2009. This marked the inception of a musical era, and the same year saw him lending his soulful voice to popular tracks like "Raabta" from Agent Vinod, "Uska Hi Banana" from 1920: Evil Returns, and "Duaa" from Shanghai.

However, it was the year 2013 that catapulted Arijit Singh to unparalleled fame. The release of the chart-topping tracks "Tum Hi Ho" and "Chahun Main Ya Naa" from Aashiqui 2 not only won hearts but also solidified his position as one of the most prominent and beloved playback singers in the Indian music industry.

Beyond national acclaim, Arijit's global impact is reflected in his consecutive recognition as the most-streamed Indian artist for the years 2020, 2021, 2022, and 2023 by Spotify. He also holds the prestigious title of the second most followed artist globally and the most followed Asian on Spotify, attesting to his universal appeal and influence in the world of music. Arijit Singh's journey is a testament to his unparalleled talent, versatility, and the ability to strike an emotional chord with audiences worldwide, making him a musical maestro celebrated across borders.
